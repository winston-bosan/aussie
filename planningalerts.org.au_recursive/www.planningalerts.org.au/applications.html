Planning approval is sought for subdivision of the subject land into seventeen (17) lots, with creation of two (2) areas of common property. Lots 101-116 are proposed for residential development, while Lot 200 is proposed to be set aside and sold to the owner of No. 20 Moodemere Street for future realignment into No. 20 Moodemere Street for formalisation of the existing access. Pre-application advice was obtained from North East Water to inform the sewer loading design and confirm a staged subdivision approach that responds to the current sewerage capacity limitations in Rutherglen. It is proposed to complete the development in three (3) stages to accommodate for current sewer servicing limits in Rutherglen. Stage one subdividing six (6) lots (Lots 101-104, Lot 107 & Lot 200), stage two subdividing eight (8) lots (Lots 105-106 & Lots 108-113) and stage three subdividing three (3) lots (Lots 114-116). Stage one will see the creation of one (1) common property area (CP1) and stage 2 will see creation of one (1) common property area (CP2). It is proposed that the existing motel, residence, pool, shed and associated outbuildings be removed in stage two for the most sustainable development of the site, ensuring that the subject land is appropriately redeveloped for the best use of the land. The proposal requests to remove exotic and planted native vegetation from the proposed site and to retain remnant native vegetation within a subdivided lot smaller than 4000m2. The subdivision design has been prepared with consideration to provision of services and appropriate access for future residents, offering an urban style living opportunity while also efficiently developing the land within proximity of the amenity of the Rutherglen township. An Infrastructure Servicing Report (Attachment 5) and Ecological Assessment Report (Attachment 6) have been prepared to support and justify the proposal
